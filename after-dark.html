Prague
After Dark

When the daytime tours end, the real Prague begins. The version most tourists never find — jazz cellars, rooftop bars with castle views, legendary cocktail haunts, and theatres that exist nowhere else on earth.

Jazz Clubs
4 venues
Jazz Club €10–20 cover Smíchov
Jazz Dock
Janáčkovo nábřeží 2, Praha 5

Prague's finest jazz venue, built over the Vltava river. Glass walls overlooking the water, world-class international and local jazz acts nightly. One of the most atmospheric rooms in Europe.

Book a river-facing table at least 48 hours ahead — they sell out every weekend without exception.
Jazz Club €8–15 cover Old Town
AghaRTA Jazz Centrum
Železná 16, Praha 1

Underground jazz in a Gothic cellar in the Old Town — intimate, dark, and legendary. Running since 1991, this is where Prague's serious jazz scene lives. Three sets nightly.

Arrive early for the 9pm set — the cellar holds only 60 people and late-comers stand.
Jazz Club €12–18 cover New Town
Reduta Jazz Club
Národní 20, Praha 1

The oldest jazz club in Central Europe — opened in 1958. Bill Clinton famously played the saxophone here during his 1994 Prague visit. History on every wall.

Ask for the table by the stage left corner — best sightlines in the house.
Jazz & Blues €8–14 cover Old Town
Ungelt Jazz & Blues Club
Týn 2, Praha 1

Hidden in the ancient Ungelt courtyard behind the Týn Church. Medieval vaulted ceilings, Czech and international acts, and an expertly curated whisky selection to accompany the music.

The Ungelt courtyard itself is stunning even before you go inside — arrive 20 minutes early to soak it in.
Rooftop Bars
3 venues
Rooftop Bar €€ Old Town
T-Anker
Náměstí Republiky 8, Praha 1

The best rooftop in Prague, full stop. Perched above Náměstí Republiky with unobstructed 360° views — Prague Castle, the Old Town rooftops, and the Powder Tower glowing at night.

The concierge platform reserves tables here — walk-ins on Friday and Saturday evenings are nearly impossible.
Sky Bar €€€ New Town
Cloud 9 Sky Bar & Lounge
Hilton Prague, Pobřežní 1

On the 9th floor of the Hilton Prague, with a wraparound terrace and indoor cocktail lounge. Sophisticated, quieter than T-Anker, perfect for business drinks or a romantic evening with castle views.

The west-facing terrace seats catch the last light of sunset over Prague Castle — arrive by 7pm in summer.
Terrace Bar € Vyšehrad
Café Citadela at Vyšehrad
V Pevnosti 159/5b, Praha 2

The hidden gem of Prague's evening scene — a terrace bar inside the Vyšehrad fortress walls, with views of the Vltava valley that most tourists never find. Remarkably good value.

Combine with the Vyšehrad cemetery visit at dusk — the fortress is magical when the crowds are gone.
Cocktail Bars
4 venues
World's Best Bars €€€ Old Town
Hemingway Bar
Karolíny Světlé 26, Praha 1

Ranked among the World's 50 Best Bars. Art Deco interior, over 200 absinthe varieties, and bartenders who treat cocktail-making as a fine art. The Czech absinthe ritual here is unmissable.

Order the Sidecar — it has been on the menu since day one and is still the best drink they make.
Speakeasy €€€ Old Town
Black Angel's Bar
Staroměstské náměstí 29, Praha 1

A 1930s-inspired prohibition bar hidden beneath the Old Town Square — exposed brick vaults, dim candlelight, and cocktails that won the Diageo World Class competition. Exceptional by any standard.

Request the tasting menu — six miniature cocktails spanning the full history of the bar's signature drinks.
Cocktail Bar €€ Vinohrady
Bitte
Mánesova 13, Praha 2

A neighbourhood cocktail bar in beautiful Vinohrady that locals fiercely guard from tourist lists. Exceptional seasonal cocktails, a remarkable natural wine selection, and the best playlist in Prague.

The bar opens at 5pm — arrive then to get a seat. By 8pm it becomes standing-room only.
Cocktail Bar €€ Žižkov
Parlour
Blanická 26, Praha 2

A beautifully designed cocktail parlour that changed the Prague cocktail scene when it opened. Serious technique, approachable menu, and a rare selection of Czech spirits not found elsewhere.

Ask the bartender what they're proud of this week — the answer is always interesting and usually delicious.
Live Shows
4 venues
Black Light Theatre €25–40 Old Town
Image Theatre
Pařížská 4, Praha 1

Prague's most acclaimed Black Light Theatre — a genre that was essentially invented in this city. Spectacular visual performances combining dance, mime, and lighting that exist nowhere else in the world.

Premium seats are worth the extra €10 — front rows get the full dimensional effect of the performance.
Classical Concert €29–55 Prague Castle
Lobkowicz Palace Concerts
Jiřská 3, Praha 1 (Castle)

Chamber concerts in the Baroque palace where Mozart himself once performed. The Lobkowicz family owns original scores by Beethoven and Handel — the same music performed in the room it was written for.

Combine with the palace museum in the afternoon — concert-goers receive a 20% museum entry discount.
Opera & Ballet €18–120 Old Town
Estates Theatre
Ovocný trh 1, Praha 1

The theatre where Mozart premiered Don Giovanni in 1787 — one of the most historically significant opera houses in the world. Still staging full-scale opera, ballet, and drama in its original 18th-century hall.

Upper-circle tickets start at €18 — excellent value for a world-class performance in a stunning historic space.
Live Music Venue €12–30 Holešovice
La Fabrika
Komunardů 30, Praha 7

A converted industrial factory in the trendy Holešovice district — Prague's best mid-sized live music and cultural venue. International touring acts, theatre productions, and some of Prague's best club nights.

Holešovice is Prague's most interesting neighbourhood right now — combine La Fabrika with dinner at any of the restaurants on Komunardů street.
