Jazz Dock

Prague's finest jazz venue, built over the Vltava river. Glass walls overlooking the water, world-class international and local jazz acts nightly. One of the most atmospheric rooms in Europe.

Jazz Club €8–15 cover Old Town
AghaRTA Jazz Centrum
Železná 16, Praha 1
Opens 8pm · 3 sets nightly Walk-ins welcome

Underground jazz in a Gothic cellar in the Old Town — intimate, dark, and legendary. Running since 1991, this is where Prague's serious jazz scene lives. Three sets nightly.

Arrive early for the 9pm set — the cellar holds only 60 people and late-comers stand.

Jazz Club €12–18 cover New Town
Reduta Jazz Club
Národní 20, Praha 1
Opens 9pm · Last entry midnight Booking required

The oldest jazz club in Central Europe — opened in 1958. Bill Clinton famously played the saxophone here during his 1994 Prague visit. History on every wall.

Ask for the table by the stage left corner — best sightlines in the house.

Jazz & Blues €8–14 cover Old Town
Ungelt Jazz & Blues Club
Týn 2, Praha 1
Opens 8pm · Last entry 1am Walk-ins welcome

Hidden in the ancient Ungelt courtyard behind the Týn Church. Medieval vaulted ceilings, Czech and international acts, and an expertly curated whisky selection to accompany the music.

The Ungelt courtyard itself is stunning even before you go inside — arrive 20 minutes early to soak it in.

Hemingway Bar

A 1930s-inspired prohibition bar hidden beneath the Old Town Square — exposed brick vaults, dim candlelight, and cocktails that won the Diageo World Class competition. Exceptional by any standard.

Black Light Theatre €25–40 Old Town
Image Theatre
Pařížská 4, Praha 1
Shows 8pm & 10pm nightly Booking required

Prague's most acclaimed Black Light Theatre — a genre that was essentially invented in this city. Spectacular visual performances combining dance, mime, and lighting that exist nowhere else in the world.

Premium seats are worth the extra €10 — front rows get the full dimensional effect of the performance.

Classical Concert €29–55 Prague Castle
Lobkowicz Palace Concerts
Jiřská 3, Praha 1 (Castle)
Shows 7pm · Book 48 hrs ahead Booking required

Chamber concerts in the Baroque palace where Mozart himself once performed. The Lobkowicz family owns original scores by Beethoven and Handel — the same music performed in the room it was written for.

Combine with the palace museum in the afternoon — concert-goers receive a 20% museum entry discount.

Opera & Ballet €18–120 Old Town
Estates Theatre
Ovocný trh 1, Praha 1
Performance times vary · Check schedule Booking required

The theatre where Mozart premiered Don Giovanni in 1787 — one of the most historically significant opera houses in the world. Still staging full-scale opera, ballet, and drama in its original 18th-century hall.

Upper-circle tickets start at €18 — excellent value for a world-class performance in a stunning historic space.

Live Music Venue €12–30 Holešovice
La Fabrika
Komunardů 30, Praha 7
Doors 7pm · Varies by show Walk-ins welcome

A converted industrial factory in the trendy Holešovice district — Prague's best mid-sized live music and cultural venue. International touring acts, theatre productions, and some of Prague's best club nights.

Holešovice is Prague's most interesting neighbourhood right now — combine La Fabrika with dinner at any of the restaurants on Komunardů street.

Underground Cellar Bar € Beer & wine only Vinohrady
U Sudu
Vodičkova 10, Praha 1
Opens 4pm · Closes 4am Walk-ins only

Prague's most surreal bar — a rabbit warren of interconnected Gothic cellars descending six levels beneath the street. Each tunnel leads to another. Locals have been getting lost here for decades. Czech beer at local prices, zero tourist markup.

Keep descending — the lowest level is the most atmospheric. Not all guests discover it exists.

Literary Dive Bar € Cash only Žižkov
Bukowski's Bar
Bořivojova 86, Praha 3
Opens 3pm · Closes 5am Walk-ins only

Named after Charles Bukowski, this Žižkov institution has no sign outside and serves some of the cheapest drinks in the city. Artists, writers, and night-owls from every walk of life — 3am conversations that go somewhere. The real Prague in one room.

There is no sign. Look for a black door with a small sticker. If you find it, you belong there.

Most unique venue in Prague
Steampunk Club € Entry from €3 Holešovice
Cross Club
Plynární 23, Praha 7
Opens 2pm · Closes 6am Walk-ins welcome

An industrial steampunk universe spanning multiple floors — the interior was built entirely from salvaged machine parts, pipes, and scrap metal by the owner over 15 years. Rotating DJ nights, live music, and a terrace in the industrial wasteland of Holešovice. Utterly unlike anything else on earth.

Explore all three levels — each has a completely different atmosphere. The top terrace is extraordinary.

Underground Bar € Local prices Nové Město
Vzorkovna
Náměstí Republiky 8 (basement), Praha 1
Opens 6pm · Closes 3am Walk-ins welcome

Literally meaning "Sample Room" — a basement bar hidden directly under the Palladium shopping mall, completely invisible from street level. Czech students, artists, and off-duty bartenders from the city's cocktail scene. No pretension, €1.50 beers, outstanding atmosphere.

The entrance is a plain door with no branding — just descend the stairs. Best on a Wednesday or Thursday.
